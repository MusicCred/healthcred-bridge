The Problem We're Solving

A Broken System — With a Clear Fix

Every year, more than 7 million individuals move through America's correctional system. The vast majority arrive uninsured — and leave uninsured. Without healthcare coverage at release, the cycle continues: untreated conditions, no follow-up care, no prescription access, and a direct pathway back to incarceration.

HealthCred operates at the exact inflection point — the transition between custody and community — where a single enrollment decision determines whether someone has healthcare access or doesn't. We don't just enroll people. We create continuity of care at the moment it matters most.

Research consistently shows that access to healthcare coverage upon release is one of the strongest predictors of reduced recidivism. HealthCred is infrastructure for that outcome.

🏥
The Jail Medical Cost Crisis
Counties bear the full cost of uninsured inmate medical care — off-site emergency visits, specialist referrals, hospitalizations — with no reimbursement pathway. HealthCred activates coverage that eliminates this liability. Lee County documented $5.9M in savings within its first year of implementation.
⚖️
Why Sheriffs Embrace This
Sheriffs and jail administrators face relentless budget pressure from avoidable medical costs. HealthCred provides a zero-cost solution that reduces that burden, creates documented fiscal impact, and supports better reentry outcomes — making it politically and operationally easy to say yes. Every facility approached has signed a contract.
🔄
Breaking the Recidivism Cycle
Healthcare coverage at reentry supports mental health treatment, substance use recovery, and chronic condition management — the three leading drivers of recidivism. HealthCred's model generates commercial returns while delivering measurable social infrastructure. The business works because the mission works.

Investor Questions

The Questions You’re
Already Asking.

What if ACA or Medicaid policy changes? +

HealthCred operates in non-Medicaid-expansion states where ACA marketplace plans are the primary vehicle. Federal ACA marketplace structure has survived two administrations, two Supreme Court challenges, and multiple congressional repeal attempts. More importantly: the commission structure is carrier-paid and carrier-driven — not government-reimbursed. Even in policy shift scenarios, carriers continue to pay commissions to brokers who enroll valid members. Our exposure is enrollment volume, not reimbursement policy.

What’s the clawback or reversal rate? +

Carrier commission clawbacks occur when a member disenrolls within 90 days of activation. HealthCred’s model mitigates this in two ways: (1) enrollment is completed during custody with activation triggered at release — members are already committed before they’re counted as active; (2) the ~80–85% renewal rate is driven by the continuity of coverage across the release transition, which is HealthCred’s core value proposition. Exact clawback data by cohort is available in the full data room upon NDA execution.

Who else is doing this? What’s the moat? +

No national competitor operates at the intersection of correctional facility contracts, ACA enrollment infrastructure, and carrier commission revenue. Prison telecom (Securus, GTL) owns communication access. Managed care (Centurion, Wellpath) owns clinical delivery. HealthCred owns the enrollment layer — which no one else is building at scale. The moat is operational: every facility approached has signed a contract. The embedded model means HealthCred is inside the facility’s workflow, not competing from the outside.

Why hasn’t HealthCred grown faster? +

Because the company has operated on $1.3M in friends-and-family capital with no debt. Every dollar deployed has been to prove the model, not scale it. The 54-facility footprint across three states, 9,200+ active lives, and $2.52M annualized run-rate on that capital base is the point — not a limitation. This raise is not proving the model. It is the growth accelerant for a model that already works.

What happens if a facility terminates the contract? +

Carrier commissions on already-enrolled lives continue regardless of facility contract status — the member relationship is between HealthCred (as broker of record) and the carrier, not contingent on the facility agreement. Facility termination affects new enrollment volume from that location, not existing active lives. Given that every facility approached to date has signed, and that the program generates documented government savings (see Lee County: $5.9M), termination risk is operationally low.

You are investing in the $5M bridge round — the first tranche of a planned $25M total raise.
